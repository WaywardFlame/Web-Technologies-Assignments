The topic I have chosen to discuss for this assignment is a video game

What is Morrowind?

The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind was an open world role playing game
released by Bethesda Game Studios in 2002 for Windows and the Xbox.
Following in the footsteps of the previous games in the series,
Morrowind unleashes the player upon a massive open world, and lets them
explore that world however they wish. The key difference from the previous
games in the series is that Morrowind uses a handcrafted world, rather
a procedurally generated one, and it managed to do so entirely with true 3D
graphics, instead of the pseudo-3D graphics of the previous games. At the
time, there nothing else quite like Morrowind's world. Yes, you had games
that tried to simulate massive worlds, such as The Legend of Zelda games,
but those typically relied on segmented worlds or massive amounts of empty
terrain to be feasible on their target platforms, even the previous Elder
Scrolls games had to use massive amounts of empty terrain. Morrowind did
not have those limitations, which, in combination with the game's genuinely
interesting setting and story, led to the game becoming a critical and
commercial success.

Why am I talking about Morrowind?

A couple of months ago now, I had played Morrowind for the first time
after developing a interest in The Elder Scrolls series again, and
because I was tired of waiting for The Elder Scrolls VI to come out.
Before Morrowind, I had only dabbled in the fourth game, Oblivion,
and I put a countless amount of time into the fifth game, Skyrim, as
well as Bethesda's other RPG series, Fallout. Despite Morrowind's
technical issues, and certain game design decisions that truly boggled
my mind, I still found it to be a rather enjoyable experience. Every
time that I thought I had had enough of the game, I found myself
coming back again and again, seeking to see more of the world, and to learn
more of a world that I knew very little about despite my time with Skyrim.
And the game's story was actually good, which surprised me given Bethesda's
recent reputation for not exactly making games with good writing.
Simply put, the game was just good fantasy, and while I don't see myself
returning to play the game again, it did make me remember why I loved
Skyrim so much, and why I very much would like another Elder Scrolls
after all this time.

Some Fun Facts About Morrowind

  1. Morrowind is older than me.
  2. Morrowind's world was somewhat remade in a later game, The Elder Scrolls Online.
    • The Elder Scrolls Online is a MMO spin off of the main series.
    • Despite being a spin off, most the series' lore comes from Elder Scrolls Online.
    • Morrowind made me finally play Elder Scrolls Online to see the world in a new light.
  3. Although it is the third main series game, Morrowind is actually the fifth game in the franchise.
